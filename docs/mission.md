# Mission

## Turn the lights on

Kōrero Mārama exists to make the exercise of public power legible. Laws decide who gains and who
loses, but they are written to be read by specialists, and their real substance is scattered — a
headline bill, the Acts it quietly amends, the regulations made underneath it, and the official
advice given before the vote. Read any one piece alone and you miss the picture.

The mission is to re-aggregate that dispersed substance into clear, sourced language, so an
ordinary person can see what a law actually does — and so power answers to the public rather than
to whoever has the patience to read the fine print.

## Accountability goal, neutral method

The goal is accountability. The **method** is deliberately neutral, and that is not a
contradiction — it is the point.

- We surface **sourced patterns**, never motive. We can show that a clause removes a remedy and
  quote the words that do it; we do not claim anyone *intended* harm.
- We **never tell anyone how to vote.** The tool helps people decide from sourced facts; it never
  answers "vote for X."
- The **same method runs on every bill**, regardless of which party introduced it.

Being boringly accurate beats being persuasive. Neutrality is both the ethic and the defence: a
tool that holds power to account has to be one that power cannot credibly dismiss.

## Why "no claim without a source" is the whole thing

A transparency tool is only as trustworthy as its weakest unsourced claim. So the project's first
principle is also its hardest constraint: nothing is surfaced that cannot be backed by the source
text and a citation. Everything else — the multi-model verification, the human review gate, the
cryptographic provenance — exists to make that promise real and checkable by anyone.
